Optional side event, traditionally held during CERN Spring Campus

Why participate?

  • Test yourself
  • Socialize
  • Have fun!
  • Win prizes!

Goal

Write an AI that will win most 1v1 duels.

Game mechanics (setup)

  • Square grid 50x50
  • Edges wrap
  • Each cell contains up to 100 troops
  • Start in a random location with 100 troops

Game mechanics (movement)

  • Prepare a sequence of commands.
  • From, Where, HowMany.
  • new MovementCommand(from, LEFT, 10)
  • No diagonals: 4 directions UP, DOWN, LEFT, RIGHT

Game mechanics (battle)

  • Enter cell with opponent - battle
  • Who has more troops - wins
  • Winner take losses, according to the number of defeated troops

Example 1

  • Player 1: 42 troops
  • Player 2: 16 troops
  • Player 1 win, but loses 16 troops

Example 2

  • Player 1: 100 troops
  • Player 2: 100 troops
  • Cell become empty

Game mechanics (information)

  • Full information

  • UniverseView.belongsToMe(x, y)
  • UniverseView.getPopulation(x, y)

Game mechanics (growth)

  • Every round you get +10% in each square have.
  • Result is rounded
  • 4 + 10% == 4 + 0.4 == 4.4 == 4
  • 5 + 10% == 5 + 0.5 == 5.5 == 6
  • Everything higher than 100 will be cut off
  • 92 + 10% == 92 + 9.2 == 101.2 == 100

Win conditions

Eliminate opponent

or

Have more troops after 2000 rounds

  • You'll find a stand-alone emulator in a bundle.
  • Implement your first PlayerBot and try it out
  • Build and upload your bot to the server, to compete against other participants.
  • Check how your bot performs. All games are accessible

Details

  • Illegal movement finishes your turn.
  • You can write statefull bots
  • No thread creation and reflexion allowed
  • You can write logs, and access them, but please keep it reasonably small (log size is limited)
  • There are timeouts for single turn (50 ms) and for bot initialization (5 sec)
